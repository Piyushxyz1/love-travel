Trip Overview

Bengal Tigers are reckoned to be the King of Indian Jungles. Living in the deep and dark woods, these Big Cats are a subject of great interest for many wildlife lovers. Tigers have always remained special to India and have been spoken about in several Indian lore and traditions. These largest members of the cat family are known for their unmatched power, and their ability to camouflage, hunt and keep large territories of jungle to themselves. It is nothing but sheer delight to witness such a majestic animal right in front of one's eyes, of course not behind a cage but in its natural habitat. Amongst the various wildlife reserves in India, Corbett National Park has been the favourite haunt for wildlife enthusiasts. Corbett is also the first national park in the country to initiate Project Tiger. Today, this national park offers a great opportunity for wildlife aficionados to stay amidst the jungle in the forest lodge and experience the thrill of being closer to these big wild cats. Recently five tigers were spotted in Corbett National Park, which is a sign of assurance that one has a fair chance of sighting tigers here.

Wildlife Safari Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival and Introduction

Arrive at the National Park where you'll be welcomed by our expert guides. After a quick briefing, we will start with an introductory safari ride to explore the park and its animals.

Day 2: Full-Day Safari

On day two, we head deeper into the park for a full-day safari. Expect to see elephants, tigers, and other incredible creatures in their natural habitats.

Day 3: Visit to the Nature Reserve

We will visit a nearby nature reserve, where we’ll explore conservation efforts and learn about the park’s unique ecosystem. You will also have time for a nature walk with a local guide.
